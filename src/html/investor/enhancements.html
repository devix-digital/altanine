Understanding Diabetes Treatment

The Role of Metformin and Beyond

Diabetes, a chronic disease affecting millions globally, disrupts carbohydrate metabolism, causing high blood glucose levels and leading to complications like heart and kidney diseases, vision problems, and neuropathy.Â 

The treatment methods for diabetes vary depending on the type of the disease. Type 1 diabetes usually requires regular insulin injections, while Type 2 diabetes can often be managed through lifestyle changes, diet, and the intake of oral hypoglycemic drugs such as Metformin.

Metformin, effective for Type 2 diabetes, lowers blood glucose and aids in weight loss but may cause gastrointestinal side effects, potentially leading to its discontinuation. Despite being generally safe, Metformin can occasionally lead to severe issues like vitamin B12 deficiency or lactic acidosis, especially in those with kidney problems.
