Metformin

What is Metformin?

Currently, over 150 million people worldwide are using metformin.

What is Metformin?

Metformin is the most prescribed drug for managing type 2 diabetes, used by over 150 million people worldwide. It's known for its efficacy in lowering blood sugar levels and is considered one of the safest pharmacological options available for diabetes treatment.

What are the common side effects of Metformin?

While Metformin is safe for most patients, up to 75% of individuals may experience gastrointestinal side effects. These can include diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal discomfort, and flatulence. The severity of these side effects varies, and they are more commonly associated with the immediate-release formulations of Metformin.

Why do some patients stop taking Metformin?

The discontinuation and nonadherence rates for Metformin can be as high as 46%, mainly due to gastrointestinal side effects. Patients are encouraged to discuss any adverse effects with their healthcare provider, as there may be alternative dosing strategies or formulations that can minimize side effects.

Can Metformin help with weight loss?

Yes, Metformin has been associated with weight loss, particularly in obese individuals with or without diabetes. On average, Metformin can reduce weight by about 2.9 kg, with the effect persisting for up to 8 years. However, weight loss in lean individuals may be considered an unintended side effect.

Are there benefits to taking Metformin beyond diabetes management?

Absolutely. Metformin offers cardiovascular benefits, such as lower rates of death due to cardiovascular disease, and may aid in weight management. It's also prescribed off-label for conditions like prediabetes, gestational diabetes, polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), and to counteract weight gain from antipsychotic medicines. Ongoing research is exploring its potential to lower cancer risk, reduce dementia and stroke rates, slow aging, prevent age-related diseases, and increase lifespan.

Can Metformin prevent diabetes in people with prediabetes?

Metformin has been shown to delay or even prevent the onset of type 2 diabetes in individuals with prediabetes, making it a valuable tool in prediabetes management.

Is Metformin used during pregnancy?

Yes, Metformin can be prescribed to manage gestational diabetes and has been used to help control blood sugar levels during pregnancy, ensuring both maternal and fetal health.
