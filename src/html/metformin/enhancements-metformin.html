How diabetes is treated today

Metformin

This is the most commonly prescribed medication for Type 2 diabetes, likely used by about 60-70% of patients, especially as a first-line treatment.

Insulin

Used by nearly all Type 1 diabetes patients and many with advanced Type 2 diabetes, accounting for approximately 25-35% of use.

Other Oral Medications

Sulfonylureas and Meglitinides, once more widely used, now have a smaller market share (around 5-10% and 1-3%, respectively). DPP-4 Inhibitors, GLP-1 Receptor Agonists, and SGLT2 Inhibitors are more recent and represent a significant portion of the market, each ranging around 10-20%.
